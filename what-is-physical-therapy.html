What Is Physical Therapy?

Physical therapy helps people move better, hurt less, and return to the activities that matter to them.

What it is

Physical therapy is a movement-based healthcare service focused on reducing pain, restoring function, and improving performance in daily life, work, and sport.

Care often includes a detailed conversation, movement assessment, hands-on treatment when useful, and a plan built around exercise, education, and practical next steps.

Who it can help

  • People dealing with new or recurring pain
  • Active adults returning to exercise, running, lifting, or sport
  • Anyone recovering from an injury or trying to avoid a setback
  • People who want a clearer understanding of what their body needs

Services Provided

1
Physical therapy evaluation
A full first-visit assessment to understand your history, symptoms, goals, and movement patterns.
2
Follow-up treatment visits
Progressive care that adapts as you improve, with treatment decisions based on response and function.
3
Movement and exercise planning
Custom exercise programs built around your current level, schedule, and the demands of your activities.
4
Pain and injury education
Straightforward explanations to help you understand what is happening and make confident decisions.
5
Return-to-activity guidance
Support for getting back to workouts, hobbies, and daily demands without guessing your way through recovery.
6
Prevention and performance support
Planning for people who want to move more efficiently, reduce flare-ups, and stay consistent long term.
